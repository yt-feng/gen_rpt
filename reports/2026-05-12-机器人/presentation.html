Key highlights

The research narrows the agenda to a few high-conviction issues

1

The global robotics market is entering a phase of accelerated growth, projected to expand at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 15-20% through 2030, driven by artificial intelligence integration, labor shortages, and the push for operational efficiency across industries. Industrial robotics remains the largest segment, but service robotics—particularly in healthcare, logistics, and agriculture—is emerging as the fastest-growing area. Asia-Pacific, led by China, dominates both production and adoption, while North America and Europe focus on high-value applications and collaborative robots. Incumbents such as ABB, Fanuc, and Yaskawa maintain strong market positions, but agile startups and tech giants are disrupting the landscape with innovative business models like Robot-as-a-Service (RaaS). Collaborative robots and autonomous mobile robots are reshaping factory and warehouse operations, lowering barriers for small and medium-sized enterprises. However, the industry faces significant risks: fragmented regulatory frameworks, cybersecurity vulnerabilities, supply chain concentration in semiconductors and rare earths, and ethical concerns around job displacement. For a company considering entry or expansion, the highest strategic fit lies in healthcare and logistics robotics, with a focus on collaborative and AI-enabled solutions, targeting the Asia-Pacific region for manufacturing and the North American market for high-value applications. A phased approach—starting with strategic partnerships or acquisitions in high-growth verticals—can mitigate risks while capturing near-term opportunities.

Approach

Seven-step problem solving structures the work before synthesis

1. Step 1Define the strategic question and decision criteria for robotics market entry or expansion.
2. Step 2Structure the analysis using an issue tree covering market overview, competitive landscape, technology, regulation, and opportunities/risks.
3. Step 3Gather data from industry reports, financial filings, patent databases, and expert interviews.
4. Step 4Apply the 10 Tests framework to validate assumptions: market size, growth rate, competitive intensity, barriers to entry, technology maturity, regulatory impact, customer adoption, business model viability, talent availability, and risk mitigation.
5. Step 5Synthesize findings into a pyramid-principle report with conclusion-first headings.
6. Step 6Develop insight cards and charts to highlight key strategic implications.
7. Step 7Formulate actionable recommendations aligned with the decision question.
